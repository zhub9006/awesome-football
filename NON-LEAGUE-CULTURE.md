# Non-League Football Match Day Culture

_A section added to the [awesome-football](https://github.com/openfootball/awesome-football) project celebrating the unique community, traditions, and fan experiences found in non-league football._

Non-league football — from the **National League** (5th tier) down through the **Isthmian League**, **Northern Premier League**, and **Southern League** — has a distinctive match day culture rooted in community spirit, accessibility, and tradition.

## Key Characteristics

### Community Atmosphere
Non-league grounds are intimate and welcoming. Fans know each other by name, creating a personal, inclusive environment far removed from the commercial feel of professional venues. Supporters traditionally gather at local pubs before and after matches.

### Match Day Traditions
Many clubs have unique rituals: specific songs and chants, pre-match gatherings, and post-match debate in the pub. The **"92 Club"** tradition involves visiting all 92 accredited football league grounds across England and Wales.

### Family-Friendly Environment
Affordable tickets (often under £15), fresh food from local chip shops, and a relaxed, safe environment where children can enjoy the game close to the pitch.

### Volunteer-Supported Clubs
Many non-league clubs are community-run, with fans directly involved through **supporters' trusts** and community ownership models.

### Strong Local Identity
Non-league clubs are deeply tied to their towns and communities, serving as focal points for local pride with fierce local rivalries.

### Vibrant Terrace Culture
Chanting, singing, and banter between fans remain authentic and energetic, preserving a raw football culture that has largely disappeared from the professional game.
