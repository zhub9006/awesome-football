# Non-League Football – Match Day Culture & Community

_Explorations of match day traditions, supporter culture, and community identity across the English non-league pyramid._

## Match Day Culture & Traditions

### Supporters' Trusts & Community Ownership
Many non-league clubs are governed by supporters' trusts ([Supporters Direct](https://www.supportersdirect.org/), [MyFootballClub](https://www.myfootballclub.co.uk/)), giving fans direct say in club governance and creating a unique ownership model distinct from the professional game.

### Intimate Ground Atmospheres
Capacities from a few hundred to a few thousand mean every voice in the stands can be heard, creating highly personal, electric match day experiences close to the pitch.

### Volunteer-Run Clubs
From chairman to kit man, many non-league clubs are run entirely by volunteers, fostering genuine community ownership and pride.

### FA Cup Giant-Killings
The annual FA Cup and FA Trophy bring national attention to non-league clubs, with famous upsets creating unforgettable match day atmospheres.

### Groundhopping Culture
An active community of groundhoppers visits and documents non-league grounds, sharing experiences on social media and dedicated forums.
