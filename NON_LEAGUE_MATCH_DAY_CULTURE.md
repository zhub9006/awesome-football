## Key Themes in Non-League Match Day Culture

- **The Social Club**: Every non-league ground has its own clubhouse or social club where the community gathers - a welcoming hub far removed from corporate hospitality
- **Pre-Match Rituals**: Pub gatherings, pre-match walks, and scarf-wearing traditions that span generations
- **Food Culture**: Legendary steak and ale pies, mash and gravy from local bakeries, and grilling food stalls as community hubs
- **Physical Programmes**: Match day programmes as collectible souvenirs supporting club finances directly
- **Terrace Freedom**: No assigned seats, freedom to move between ends, unfiltered proximity to the action
- **Community Volunteerism**: Fans who mow pitches, sell tickets, and steward matches - the backbone of non-league football
- **Generational Traditions**: Family legacies passed through generations with season tickets spanning decades
- **Digital Integration**: Modern fans bridging traditional atmosphere with live stats, podcasts, and social media
