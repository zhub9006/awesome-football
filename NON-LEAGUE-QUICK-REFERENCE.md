# Non-League Match Day Culture — Quick Reference Card

> A concise companion reference for the 12 key non-league match day traditions, with community source links and top grounds recommendations.

---

## The 12 Key Traditions

| # | Tradition | Summary |
|---|-----------|--------|
| 1 | The Pub Signal | Pre-match gatherings at local pubs; fans, officials, and players mingle |
| 2 | Intimate Grounds | Small stadiums (500–5,000) putting supporters pitchside |
| 3 | Freedom of the Terrace | No assigned seats; stand anywhere; change ends at half-time |
| 4 | The Clubhouse / Social Club | Pre-match community hub with cheap drinks and genuine belonging |
| 5 | Pie, Mash & Gravy | Local bakery pies (£3–4) — the iconic non-league culinary ritual |
| 6 | The Physical Programme | Collectible souvenir (£2–3) supporting club finances |
| 7 | Volunteer Spirit | Fans steward, maintain pitch, paint stands, run the club |
| 8 | Local Rivalries | Community-rooted derbies with deep local identity |
| 9 | Family Inclusion | Children on pitch, £5–15 tickets, no-fuss welcome |
| 10 | Club Chants & Songs | Organic, locally-written songs passed down through generations |
| 11 | The Conference Legacy | 1979–2004 era culture of independence and self-governance |
| 12 | Non-League Day | Annual event encouraging higher-tier fans to visit local sides |

## Top Recommended Grounds to Visit

1. **FC Halifax Town** — FSA Away Day Experience Award 2025 winner; passionate fanbase
2. **Torquay United** — FSA winner; classic coastline ground with strong community
3. **Lewes FC** — FSA winner; Peace Ground with intimate atmosphere
4. **Falmouth Town** — FSA winner; small but fiercely supported Cornish club
5. **Dulwich Hamlet** — Champion Hill; vibrant away culture and community-owned club
6. **AFC Wimbledon** — Plough Lane; community-owned club with strong terrace culture
