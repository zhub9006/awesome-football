## 12 Key Non-League Match Day Traditions

| # | Tradition | In A Nutshell |
|---|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | **The Pub Signal** | Pre-match pub gatherings where fans, managers & chairmen mingle before kickoff |
| 2 | **Intimate Grounds** | Small terraced stadiums putting supporters right next to the pitch — no barriers, no VAR |
| 3 | **Volunteer Spirit** | Clubs run by volunteers — shared ownership and genuine community feel |
| 4 | **Local Rivalries** | Geographically close clubs with community-rooted derbies — "the whole town stops to watch" |
| 5 | **Family Inclusion** | Kids on the pitch at full-time, cheap/free admission for all ages |
| 6 | **The Conference Legacy** | 1979–2004 Football Conference era culture of independence and self-governance |
| 7 | **Chants & Songs** | Organic, locally-written songs reflecting community identity — not copied from the top flight |
| 8 | **The Clubhouse / Social Club** | Pre-match hub where fans, officials & players mingle over cheap drinks |
| 9 | **Pie, Mash & Gravy** | The iconic "Footy Scran" — legendary steak & ale pies from local bakeries |
| 10 | **The Physical Programme** | A couple of pounds for a collectible programme that directly supports club finances |
| 11 | **Freedom of the Terrace** | No assigned seats — stand wherever you like, switch ends at half-time |
| 12 | **Non-League Day** | Annual event (international break) encouraging higher-division fans to visit local non-league sides |
