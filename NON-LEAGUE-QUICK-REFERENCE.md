# Non-League Match Day Culture — Quick Reference

A concise companion reference card for the 12 traditions of non-league match day culture.

---

## Quick Reference: 12 Non-League Match Day Traditions

| # | Tradition | Key Detail |
|---|-----------|------------|
| 1 | **The Pub Signal** | Match day begins at the local pub — cask ale, catching up, walking to the ground together |
| 2 | **Intimate Ground Atmospheres** | Capacities from hundreds to thousands; every voice heard, close to the pitch |
| 3 | **Freedom of the Terrace** | Stand anywhere, change ends at half-time, no assigned seats, no barriers |
| 4 | **Pie, Mash, and Gravy** | Legendary steak & ale pies with creamy mash — the iconic non-league meal |
| 5 | **The Physical Programme** | Paper programmes for a couple of pounds — local history, manager notes, player interviews |
| 6 | **The Clubhouse** | Community social club where fans, officials, and players mingle freely |
| 7 | **Supporters' Trusts** | Fan governance through Supporters Direct, MyFootballClub — real ownership power |
| 8 | **Volunteer-Run Clubs** | From chairman to kit man — entirely volunteer-run, genuine community pride |
| 9 | **Local Rivalries** | Fierce, personal, organic rivalries rooted in geography and history |
| 10 | **FA Cup Giant-Killings** | Annual FA Cup/Trophy upsets create electric, unforgettable atmospheres |
| 11 | **Groundhopping** | Active community visiting and documenting non-league grounds across England |
| 12 | **Non-League Day** | Annual awareness event celebrating grassroots football match day culture |
